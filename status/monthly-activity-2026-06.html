Why this requires scrutiny

Speed at this scale changes the nature of review. When an AI can delete 48,000 lines and reshape 386 files in a month, the risk isn't slow progress — it's fast, confident, wrong progress that looks finished. So every change here goes through deliberate human scrutiny:

  • Volume outruns intuition. No one eyeballs 62,000 changed lines. Automated tests, compliance gates, and regression suites run on every change so nothing ships on trust alone.
  • Deletion is the dangerous edit. Removing code is where working behavior silently disappears. Large deletions get the closest look — what was it doing, and is it truly redundant?
  • Confident ≠ correct. AI produces plausible, well-formatted output that can still be subtly wrong. Findings are verified against real behavior, not just accepted because they compile.
  • Every fix names its test. A change isn't "done" until a failing-first test proves the bug existed and now passes — the record of scrutiny, not just the fix.
  • Deployment is separate from writing. Code committed is not code shipped; changes are built, verified, and only then deployed to customers.

The takeaway for you: this velocity is real, and it is governed. You get the speed of AI with the discipline of engineering review layered on top.
